<h2>v0.11.0 — Multi-pane reliability + scrollback fixes</h2>
<p>Heavy stability pass on tabs, splits, and resizing — every reported case of "history disappears", "new tab clones the active one", "split wipes content", "blank pane after closing a split", and "claude welcome banner stacking 4× in scrollback" is fixed.</p>
<ul>
  <li><b>New tab is independent.</b> Previously a new tab silently shared the previous tab's NSView because SwiftUI couldn't tell two single-pane PaneLayouts apart — now all tabs are mounted at all times in a ZStack with the inactive ones parked off-screen, so switching tabs preserves every pane's scrollback with zero re-parenting cost.</li>
  <li><b>Split-close no longer leaves a blank pane.</b> Root cause was Termy calling <code>scrollTo(row: Int.max/2)</code>, which SwiftTerm doesn't clamp — that poisoned <code>yDisp</code> and made every visible line draw off-screen. Removed.</li>
  <li><b>Focus actually moves after split / ⌘T.</b> The previous focus handler gated on <code>shouldClaimFocus</code>, which refuses to displace another TerminalView — so keystrokes kept landing in the OLD pane after every split. Tab/pane switch is now authoritative and takes focus unconditionally; the .onChange that should have fired on activePaneId never did (SwiftUI doesn't observe sub-object @Published changes through a parent), so a notification was added.</li>
  <li><b>Scrollback bumped 500 → 10 000 lines.</b> SwiftTerm's 500-line default was getting blown through immediately whenever a split halved the pane width — every wide line wrapped 2-3× and the oldest history fell off the bottom of the buffer. 10 000 covers hours of work.</li>
  <li><b>TUI scrollback pollution on resize is fixed.</b> Apps that don't use alt-screen (claude in particular) redraw their entire UI on every SIGWINCH — that previously left stacked copies of the welcome banner cascading down scrollback after every split / drag-resize. Termy now sends <code>CSI 3 J</code> (erase scrollback) when the pane changes by more than 30 % in either dimension, both on programmatic resizes AND on user drag-resize end. Setting <code>termy.clearScrollbackOnSignificantResize</code> in defaults disables it if you want full history preservation.</li>
  <li><b>Cinema mode actually paces output now.</b> The cps slider was being clamped to 30 minimum and then defeated by a per-tick "auto catch-up" that divided the queue by 8 — a 200-character paragraph rendered in under a second regardless of setting. Drain logic rewritten to honour cps; tiny echo bursts of typed characters bypass the pacer so typing doesn't feel laggy.</li>
  <li><b>Crash fixed: arithmetic overflow in <code>Buffer.isCursorInViewPort</code>.</b> SwiftTerm's formula (<code>yBase + 2·yDisp</code>) overflowed with deep scrollback; we stopped calling it.</li>
  <li><b>Idle timer tears down when the pane settles</b> instead of ticking forever — saves a wakeup per pane per second indefinitely.</li>
  <li><b>Install instructions clarified</b> for the Gatekeeper "Apple could not verify" warning that hits every first-time downloader — the one-line <code>xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine</code> bypass is now front-and-centre in the README.</li>
</ul>
